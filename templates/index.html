Interdisciplinary Scope

Orthopedics

Orthopedics is a medical specialty focused on the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of conditions and injuries related to the musculoskeletal system. The musculoskeletal system includes bones, joints, muscles, ligaments, tendons, and nerves that support the body's movement and provide structural support.

Diaginostic

Diagnostic procedures are medical tests or examinations conducted to determine the presence, cause, or extent of a disease, condition, or injury. These procedures help healthcare professionals make accurate diagnoses and develop appropriate treatment plans

Psycology

Psychology is the scientific study of the human mind and behavior. It is a field that explores various aspects of human cognition, emotion, perception, motivation, personality, and social interactions. Psychologists seek to understand individual and group behavior, mental processes, and the factors that influence them.

General Treatment

Details about the project

LMCI Scan

Late Mild Cognitive Impairment. This scan is likely used to evaluate individuals who are in the late stages of mild cognitive impairment, which is a condition that may progress to Alzheimer's disease. The purpose of this scan is to assess the brain's structure and function, typically using neuroimaging techniques such as magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) or positron emission tomography (PET).

AD Scan

Alzheimer's Disease. An AD scan refers to diagnostic imaging techniques used to identify changes in the brain associated with Alzheimer's disease. These scans can include MRI, PET, or other specialized imaging methods, such as amyloid PET scans that detect the accumulation of amyloid plaques or tau PET scans that detect abnormal tau protein deposition in the brain

CN Scan

CN stands for Cognitiely Normal. A CN scan is likely used as a baseline or reference scan for individuals who do not exhibit any cognitive impairment or neurological disorders. It helps establish a normal brain structure and function, serving as a point of comparison for scans conducted on individuals with cognitive disorders.

EMCI Scan

EMCI stands for Early Mild Cognitive Impairment. This scan is typically performed on individuals who are in the early stages of mild cognitive impairment, which is considered a precursor to Alzheimer's disease. It assesses brain structure and function to detect any abnormalities that may indicate the onset of the disease.
